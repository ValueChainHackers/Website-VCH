Project Overview

Li-Monti Maps is building the 'Google Maps' of lithium-ion battery recycling - a digital platform that connects manufacturers, recyclers, collectors, and other stakeholders in the battery lifecycle ecosystem. The platform helps visualize and navigate the complex network of battery recycling infrastructure.

By mapping the entire ecosystem, Li-Monti Maps makes it easier for stakeholders to find partners, understand material flows, and identify opportunities to close the loop on battery recycling.

Platform Features

Stakeholder Mapping

Visual mapping of all players in the battery recycling ecosystem and their relationships.

Network Visualization

Interactive visualization of material flows and recycling networks.

AI Matching

AI-powered system to connect stakeholders with complementary needs and capabilities.

The Challenge

The lithium-ion battery recycling ecosystem is fragmented and complex, with many stakeholders operating in silos. Manufacturers, recyclers, collectors, and policymakers often struggle to find each other and understand how to work together effectively.

Li-Monti Maps addresses this by creating a centralized platform where all stakeholders can discover each other, understand their roles, and identify opportunities for collaboration - ultimately making battery recycling more efficient and accessible.
